Developer handover · HTML + CSS + JS

POS checkout — fix the scroll, add branch transfer

Two changes to the existing production Point of Sale register: (1) a cashier can reach customer name, phone and payment method without scrolling — critical on the smaller/shorter screens common in the field, and (2) a cashier can transfer a product's stock to another branch straight from the catalog.

Screen POS register — cart panel Type Layout fix + new feature Fidelity High — CSS below is drop-in Stack Plain HTML/CSS/JS — framework-agnostic

Problem

Three stacked full-width fields push Pay off-screen

In production, once an amount is entered the cart panel reveals Customer name, then Phone Number, then Payment Method + Amount — three separate full-height rows stacked above the Pay button. On a laptop or short tablet viewport that's roughly 200px more content than fits, so the cashier must scroll the whole right panel just to tap Pay. That's a real cost per transaction for high-volume, low-friction cash sales — exactly the case that should be fastest.

Why this matters here: most Flipper sales are quick walk-in cash sales. Forcing a scroll before every one of them, on devices that are often small/older, adds friction to the single most repeated action in the app.

Fix 1 of 2

Pin the action bar · collapse customer capture to one row

Two structural changes, independent of each other:

  1. Make the cart panel a flex column with one scrolling region. Header, customer capture and the totals/payment/action footer are fixed; only the line-items list scrolls. The footer — total, payment method + amount, quick-cash, Tickets/Pay — is always in view, no matter how many lines are in the cart or whether customer fields are expanded.
  2. Collapse customer name/phone into the search row. Today "Search Customer" and the Name/Phone fields are two separate, simultaneously-stacked blocks — redundant, and expensive in height. Default to the single search row (covers the common walk-in case at zero extra cost). Tapping Add customer swaps it, in place, for one compact row with Name and Phone side by side. No new vertical space is added to the layout — it's a swap, not an insertion.

Compare: the panel on the left is today's stacked behavior (shortened to fit a small screen, scaled to this doc — the cut-off/scrollbar is the actual bug). The panel on the right is the fix, at the same height. Try the buttons — they're live.

The Mode switch is the whole answer to "how do I get from checkout to transfer": it's right at the top of the same panel, always visible, and it's the only way in or out of Transfer mode — no separate screen. Tap Transfer to see the customer/payment fields swap for a branch picker and product chips; tap 👤+ in Sale mode to expand Name/Phone; tap + add demo line a few times to prove the footer never moves.

Fix 2 of 2

Transfer a product's stock to another branch

There is currently no way to move a product's stock from the current branch to another one without leaving the register. Rather than a separate one-off dialog, transfer reuses the exact same "build a cart" interaction as a sale — the Mode switch in the panel above turns the cart panel from Sale to Transfer: pick a destination branch once, then tap products to add them just like building a sale (same steppers, same remove), and the footer's payment fields are replaced by a single Transfer action. No new mental model for the cashier — it's the same cart, a different destination and a different final button.

↑ Try it in the live panel above — flip Sale/Transfer and tap the product swatches.

Where this lives elsewhere: transfers should show up in a Transfers log (new, or a tab under Transactions) with status Pending → Received, and the destination branch should see it as incoming stock to accept — mirroring how Stock Recount already models branch-scoped inventory events.

Interaction spec
ElementBehavior
Add customer (👤+)Swaps the search row for a Name + Phone row, in place — no layout shift below it. Autofocus Name. ✕ reverts to search and clears both fields.
Cart panelFlex column: header + customer capture + footer are fixed height; only the line-item list is a scroll container (flex:1;min-height:0;overflow-y:auto). This is the whole fix — no JS required for it.
Payment method + amountStay combined on one row, immediately under Grand Total — never gated behind customer entry.
Sale / Transfer toggleSegmented switch in the cart header. Switching to Transfer swaps customer-capture for a "To branch" selector and swaps the payment footer for a single Transfer action — the line-item list and its add/remove/qty interactions are unchanged.
Transfer — adding itemsTapping a product adds it to the transfer list exactly like adding to a sale cart (same stepper, same remove). Quantity is clamped to 1…stock at the source branch.
Transfer confirmDecrements the source branch's stock immediately (optimistic) for every line; shows a toast; the receiving branch sees a pending transfer.

Reference

Tokens used (already in pos.css / onboarding/styles.css)

No new tokens were introduced — the fix reuses the existing Flipper POS palette: --blue, --ink-1…4, --line/--line-strong, --surface/--surface-2, radii --r-md (14px), shadows --sh-1/--sh-2/--sh-blue, and the Geist/Geist Mono type pairing (mono for all numerics).

Reference

Acceptance checklist
  • On a 1280×720 (or smaller) viewport, Pay is reachable with zero scrolling, cart empty or full, customer fields collapsed or expanded.
  • Expanding customer name/phone does not change the position of the total/payment/action footer.
  • Only the line-items list scrolls internally when the cart has many lines.
  • Switching Sale → Transfer and back preserves the underlying cart-panel chrome (header, footer position) — only the customer/payment fields swap for branch-select/Transfer.
  • Transfer quantity per line cannot exceed on-hand stock at the source branch.
  • A confirmed transfer is visible as pending on the destination branch's side.
